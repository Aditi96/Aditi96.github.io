A Little Kindness...

A little kindness...

A little kindness is all we need.

In a world where trust is a big deal, a little reassurance is all we need.

In a world where we love breaking up, a little patience is all we need.

In a world where soldiers are pelted stones, a little gratitude is all we need.

In a world where sex outside marriage is no big deal, a little equality is all we need.

In a world where hurting gives us pleasure, a little compassion is all we need

In a world where we need medications to sleep, a little hope is all we need

In a world where censorship is the norm, a little freedom is all we need.

In a world of too many strangers, a little smile is all we need.

In a world where we are always scared, a little courage is all we need.

In a world where hash-tags define us, a little living is all we need.

In a world where kids are the victims of terror, a little conscience is all we need.

In a world where suicide is an option, a little hug is all we need.

In a world where animals are more humane, a little humanity is all we need.

In a world to too many wrongs, a little right is all we need.

In a world of too many, a little of everything is all we need.

Home

Home

Standing at the porch, she could see her mother packing her bags. Clean and neatly folded clothes, medicines, a big box of sweets, and a picture of Maa Durga who would be her companion in the journey. All packed. All set.

A gentle breeze carrying with it the smell of her favorite dishes filled the hall. Wish she could stay here a bit longer, just a little bit.

With the sun setting and carrying on its journey to the other side of the world, she left her home with a kiss on her forehead. Her father’s strong arms helped her with the luggage.

She was all alone now. The canopy, the warmth, the smell of her home... all gone. She was on her own, independant.

Reaching her hostel, she felt happy. Friends, fun, laughter filled the corridor.

Opening her luggage, she felt at ease. It was the smell of her clean clothes and sweets, It smelt of her home; With a small note inside :

“ I knew you would forget things. Keep the clothes neatly, I have given you a packet of napthalene, Use it. Your delicates are in the upper zip. The medicines have been grouped according to the ailment. It is in a black bag between your clothes. I have packed you a box of chocolates, along with the sweets in a red polythene. It is in the bottom left corner of your bag. Share it with your friends. And in your hand bag you can find your dinner. Eat well.
I Love You."

She simply smiled to herself. The note and her mother's love in a box made her feel at home again.
